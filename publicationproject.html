PUB332 PUBLICATION PROJECT

For the course PUB332: Graphic Design in Transition: Print and Digital Periodicals, I was tasked with creating a pitch for a magazine publication concept, as well as creating a cover design, feature editorial design, web design mockups, and advertisement mockups. This project spanned the entire semester of Spring 2021 and simulated the process of establishing a magazine publication in the field.

publication concept

I wanted my magazine brand to cover film topics- for instance, film reviews, upcoming feature films, exclusive interviews, and opinion pieces around the social climate involving film. I was required to make an editorial mandate, readership guide, and a basic branding idea for my pitch.

The brand name I chose for my publication was XCU, a common term used for cinematography. XCU stands for extreme close-up, and I thought this was a clever play on words in regards to what content I wanted to include within this publication: an extreme close up on all things film.

I wanted to have a vintage look for the overall magazine, something that mirrors the film aesthetic of old cinema. In this initial pitch, I wanted to use sepia tones to create an old-timey tone similar to black-and-white films and utilize film stills to their potential and really focus on the cinematography shots as means to show an appreciation for these details.

I later looked into making a Pinterest board to create a proper moodboard to take inspiration from.

With this moodboard, I decided to stray further from the original sepia tone and instead adopted a colourful palette. I mainly took inspiration from old Hollywood movie posters with the use of exaggerated photo composition and bold, bright colours. I found many existing film publications followed a modern art direction, and I wanted to move backwards- I wanted to make the publication itself look like something out of old Hollywood. I wanted to keep the art direction playful, and take a fun twist on existing films with bright, high-contrast images with a touch of text that appears to pop out of the page.

Something a little cheesy, if you will.

sketches and thumbnail layouts

I've never really done an actual magazine layout before, so I did want to look at existing designs to take inspiration from. For the most part, I wanted to utilize images to the fullest, and use bright colours in tangent with images to have them really look like they're moving across the page.

Again, for my layout sketches, I wanted to emphasize images by using big, bold images in combination with large lettering. I was really drawn to the large signboards used for many old-fashioned theatres with vertical text and I wanted to try my hand with something similar for my main feature editorial design.

cover design

To keep the aesthetic of old film, I also decided to have covers follow a similar pattern. Although I don't want to mirror a movie poster design, I still wanted the vibe of one, so I chose to only feature one feature story instead of having one main cover story and a few sub-features.

Above, I have three thumbnail designs for potential covers. I wanted to see how certain text layouts would look and so I can have a good idea for what images I wanted to use. I did include spaces where I can include additional sub-features in case I wanted to explore that avenue.

I wasn't sure how I wanted to approach the cover design at first, I knew I wanted to have it look similar to a movie poster, but I wasn't sure what direction I wanted to go towards. So I began with two designs I thought would incorporate a more aged design style.

With the first mockup, I followed a more simple approach to text design, using only Helvetica for the feature story title. I had also doctored the image with filters in order to have a more grainy, film-like appearance. However, with this approach the cover appeared somber and more serious, definitely not the mood I wanted to go with.

With the second cover mockup, I had chosen to include a border and cut out parts of the border in order to make parts of the image really pop out. For the image, I used a filter to make it look as it were illustrated- this was inspired by older movie posters as they were often illustrated instead of photographed. The title was actually something I saw from Golden Age Superman comics; they often used 3D block text to make the superhero name punch through the page, it looked cheesy, and I was looking for cheesy.

But I still wasn't satisfied with the cover. I felt the painterly texture I applied to the image takes away from the details of the photo itself. It was really lackluster. Instead of using a texture, I decided to focus more on saturation and contrast to create that film aesthetic.

Although this wasn't mandatory, I also designed an alternative cover to show how another issue would appear.

feature editorial design

The text I used for the editorial story was actually parts of a Rolling Stone interview with Emma Stone. Please note that this was a requirement for this project to use existing text and not placeholder text (Lorem ipsum).

I used a different approach for the main article design. Of course, I couldn't put a moving picture onto the page, but I wanted to have the feeling of movement across the page. So I clipped out an image of Emma Stone and laid it so it appeared that she was leaping out of the page. Other than a still from a movie, I could clip out images to have it look like there was movement through the images. Similar to the cover image, I edited the images to have higher contrast and saturation.

The main title was inspired by old theatre and truck-stop signs. I did add a twist to this design, though. While I did want to keep older design concepts to match my moodboard, I decided to include more modern, minimalistic design as a mish-mash of times. Even though I want the title to stand out, I didn't want it to clash with the images and have the overall layout look too busy.

additional content

In addition to the cover and the main editorial, I had also designed potential web designs, web advertisements, and a blow-in subscription card.

final thoughts

This project was really fun! This was a really different avenue from other design projects I have done because I was able to experiment with a different styles and maneuver my way through art direction. I found a lot of personal growth in my own design ethos and I even find myself adopting these concepts into my illustrations and photography as well.
